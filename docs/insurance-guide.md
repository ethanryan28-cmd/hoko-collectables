# Insurance Guide

Insurance considerations for HOKO Collectables. **Disclaimer: not professional insurance advice — consult a broker for your specific situation.**

---

## Why insurance matters for a collectables business

- Stock (sealed product + graded slabs) can represent significant value in a small space
- Standard home contents insurance often excludes business stock or limits it to very low values
- A single lost or damaged parcel of high-value slabs can cause a material loss
- Fire, theft, or flood at home/storage could wipe out inventory entirely

---

## Types of insurance relevant to HOKO

### 1. Business contents / stock insurance
Covers your stock against loss, damage, theft, fire, flood.

**Key questions to ask a broker:**
- Does it cover stock held at home?
- Does it cover stock in transit (to/from graders, conventions)?
- What is the per-item limit? (Some policies have low per-item caps)
- Are graded slabs covered at their graded value or replacement cost?
- Are there exclusions for 'collectables' or 'trading cards'? (Some policies exclude these specifically)

### 2. Transit / shipping insurance
Covers parcels lost or damaged in transit.

**Options:**
- **Australia Post Extra Cover:** available at time of lodgement; covers up to $5,000 per item (check current limits at auspost.com.au)
  - Must be declared at time of lodgement — cannot be added retroactively
  - Not all items are covered — check exclusions
- **Third-party parcel insurance:** companies like ParcelGuard or Sendle offer per-shipment or subscription insurance
- **Buyer's premium insurance:** some platforms (eBay) offer buyer-side protection — not seller protection

**Recommendation for HOKO:**
- Australia Post Extra Cover for items over $100
- Consider a business transit policy if monthly shipping volume is high

### 3. Public liability insurance
Covers claims if someone is injured at your place of business.

**Relevant for HOKO if:**
- You conduct buyback meetings at home (low risk, but possible)
- You attend card shows and conventions as a seller (many shows require vendors to have public liability)

**Minimum:** $10M public liability is standard for market/convention vendors.

### 4. Professional indemnity insurance
Covers claims arising from advice you give professionally.
Generally not required for a retail/resale business like HOKO.

## Shipping — practical recommendations

| Item value | Shipping recommendation |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Under $50 | Standard tracked post, no extra cover |
| $50-$200 | Express Post + Australia Post Extra Cover |
| $200-$500 | Express Post + Extra Cover + signature on delivery |
| Over $500 | Express Post + Extra Cover + signature + notify buyer |
| Over $1,000 | Consider courier (StarTrack/DHL) with declared value insurance |

**Always:**
- Keep all postage receipts (they prove you shipped)
- Screenshot the tracking page showing 'delivered' for high-value orders
- For Extra Cover: keep the lodgement receipt — required for claims

## Grading submissions

When submitting cards to PSA, BGS, or CGC:
- Ship with declared value (required for insurance coverage)
- Use a tracked, insured service: Australia Post Express + Extra Cover or courier with declared value
- PSA and BGS have their own insurance while cards are in their custody — check current coverage levels on their websites
- For very high-value submissions (over $5,000 total), consider dedicated fine art / collectables transit insurance

---

## Home-based business considerations

- Your existing home and contents policy likely **does not cover business stock**
- Even if it does, limits are usually $500-$2,000 for 'business equipment'
- Notify your home insurer if running a business from home — failure to disclose can void your policy
- A business package policy is cleaner and often not much more expensive than extending a home policy

## Getting a quote — what to tell a broker

When approaching an insurance broker, have ready:
- Estimated total stock value (replacement cost)
- Where stock is stored (home, storage unit, warehouse)
- Whether you attend markets/conventions
- Annual shipping volume (roughly)
- Any single item values over $1,000
- Whether you conduct buyback meetings

**Useful resources:**
- Compare business insurance: bizcover.com.au
- Australia Post Extra Cover: auspost.com.au/sending/insurance-and-extra-cover
- Small business insurance guide: ato.gov.au (search 'business insurance deductions')

## Tax deductibility

Insurance premiums for business purposes are generally tax-deductible as a business expense.
- Business stock insurance: fully deductible
- Transit insurance on sales: deductible (cost of sale)
- Public liability for convention appearances: deductible
- Mixed-use policy (personal + business): only the business portion is deductible

Keep all insurance invoices for your records (see `docs/tax-and-gst-guide.md`).
