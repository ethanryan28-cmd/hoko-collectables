# Tax and GST Guide

**Important disclaimer:** This is a general guide only — not professional tax advice. Always consult a registered tax agent or accountant for your specific situation.

Last reviewed: June 2026

---

## GST basics for a small AU business

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is 10% charged on most sales in Australia.

**You must register for GST if:**
- Your business GST turnover (gross income) is $75,000 AUD or more in a 12-month period
- Or you expect to exceed $75,000 in the next 12 months

**You can voluntarily register before $75K if you want to:**
- Claim GST credits on business purchases (reduces your cost of goods)
- Look more established to suppliers

**If you are NOT registered for GST:**
- Do not charge GST on your sales
- Cannot claim GST credits on purchases
- Still need to lodge an income tax return and pay income tax on profits

## What sales are taxable for HOKO?

| Product type | GST status |
|-------------|-----------|
| Sealed TCG product (new) | Taxable — 10% GST applies |
| Graded slabs (PSA/BGS/CGC) | Taxable — 10% GST applies |
| Secondhand / used goods | May be GST-free or reduced — check with accountant |
| International sales (export) | GST-free if goods physically leave Australia |

> **Note:** Most domestic sales by HOKO (sealed product, graded slabs shipped within AU) are standard-rated taxable supplies.

## What purchases can you claim GST credits on?

If you are GST-registered, you can claim back the 10% GST on business purchases. This is called an **input tax credit**.

**Eligible business purchases (examples):**
- Stock purchased from Australian GST-registered suppliers
- Postage and packaging supplies
- eBay/Shopify fees (they may issue a tax invoice with GST)
- Business equipment (lightbox, phone stand, camera accessories)
- Grading fees paid to local agents (if GST is charged)

**NOT eligible:**
- Personal expenses
- Stock purchased from overseas suppliers (import — different rules apply)
- Stock purchased from private sellers (not registered for GST)

## BAS (Business Activity Statement)

Once GST-registered, you lodge a BAS with the ATO quarterly (or monthly if large turnover).

**BAS shows:**
- Total GST collected on sales (you owe this to the ATO)
- Total GST paid on purchases (the ATO owes you this as a credit)
- Net GST payable = GST collected minus GST credits

**Quarterly BAS due dates:**
| Quarter | Period | Due date |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Q1 | Jul–Sep | 28 October |
| Q2 | Oct–Dec | 28 February |
| Q3 | Jan–Mar | 28 April |
| Q4 | Apr–Jun | 28 July |

> Lodge online via myGov / ATO Business Portal or through your accountant.

## Record-keeping requirements

The ATO requires you to keep business records for **5 years** from when you lodge your tax return.

**What to keep for HOKO:**
- All purchase invoices (supplier invoices, eBay purchase history, grading receipts)
- All sales records (eBay/Shopify/Whatnot payout reports)
- Postage receipts (Australia Post receipts or MyPost Business statements)
- Bank statements showing business transactions
- Buyback records (what you paid, to whom, for what)
- Consignment records (if Phase 2 is active)

**Recommended system:**
- Export monthly payout reports from eBay, Shopify, and Whatnot
- Save all purchase invoices as PDFs in a folder by month
- Use a simple spreadsheet (or Xero/MYOB) to log income and expenses
- Reconcile against bank statement monthly

## Income tax basics

Whether or not you're GST-registered, you pay income tax on your net profit.

**Net profit = Revenue minus Cost of Goods Sold minus Business Expenses**

**Deductible business expenses (examples):**
- Cost of goods sold (what you paid for stock)
- Postage and packaging
- Platform fees (eBay final value fees, Shopify subscription)
- Business phone (proportion used for business)
- Home office (proportion of rent/utilities if working from home)
- Grading fees
- Business insurance
- Advertising and marketing costs
- Accountant/bookkeeper fees

**NOT deductible:**
- Personal expenses
- Fines and penalties
- Private use portion of mixed-use expenses

## Sole trader vs company structure

HOKO Collectables is currently operating as a sole trader (assumed). Key points:

- Business income is taxed at your personal income tax rate (up to 47% including Medicare levy at high incomes)
- Under $18,200 gross income: no income tax (tax-free threshold)
- $18,201–$45,000: 19% tax rate
- $45,001–$120,000: 32.5% tax rate
- Consider a company structure once net profit regularly exceeds $80-100K — company tax rate is 25% for small businesses
- Talk to an accountant before changing structure

## Practical checklist — quarterly (BAS time)

- [ ] Total all sales for the quarter (eBay + Shopify + Whatnot + any cash sales)
- [ ] Calculate GST collected (total sales x 1/11 if GST-registered)
- [ ] Total all GST-eligible purchases for the quarter
- [ ] Calculate GST credits (sum of GST amounts on valid tax invoices)
- [ ] Lodge BAS via ATO Business Portal or through accountant
- [ ] Pay net GST owing by due date

## When to get an accountant

Get an accountant (or at minimum a bookkeeper) when:
- You approach or exceed $75K turnover (GST registration required)
- You're unsure whether to register for GST early
- You want to claim home office deductions
- You're considering changing business structure
- You have overseas purchases or export sales
- Tax time is stressful and you're not sure what's deductible

- ATO Small Business Hub: ato.gov.au/businesses-and-organisations/starting-registering-or-closing-a-business
- myGov for lodging BAS: my.gov.au
