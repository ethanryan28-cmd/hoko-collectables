# Packaging Guide — HOKO Collectables

> Standard for how every order leaves the door. Consistent packaging = fewer complaints, fewer returns, and repeat buyers.  
> "Careful packing" is part of the brand promise — this is how we deliver it.

---

## Guiding principle

Every buyer should open their parcel and think: "This was packed by someone who actually cares about this stuff."

Sealed product and graded slabs are valuable. Pack them like you'd pack something for yourself.

---

## 1. Graded Slabs

### Materials needed
- Bubble wrap (small-cell, minimum 1 full wrap around the slab)
- Rigid cardboard mailer (not a satchel — slabs need rigidity)
- Packing tape
- Optional: small foam pad or tissue paper to fill any void space

### Method

1. **Wrap the slab** in at least one full layer of bubble wrap — bubbles facing inward (protects the case from scratching)
2. **Double wrap** for slabs over $200 or any PSA 10 / BGS 9.5+
3. Place in a **rigid cardboard mailer** sized so the slab fits snugly — minimal movement inside
4. If there's void space, fill with scrunched paper or additional bubble wrap (no polystyrene peanuts — they shift)
5. Seal with packing tape on all flaps
6. Mark the outside: **FRAGILE** (stamp or written label)
7. Include the **HOKO10 package insert card** before sealing

### What NOT to do
- Never use a soft satchel for slabs — they'll flex and the case can crack
- Never let the slab slide freely inside the box
- Never stack multiple heavy items directly on top of a slab

---

## 2. Sealed Booster Boxes (ETBs, Full Boxes)

### Materials needed
- Double-wall cardboard box (or original manufacturer box if available)
- Bubble wrap or foam padding
- Packing tape (heavy-duty)

### Method

1. **Wrap the sealed product** in at least one layer of bubble wrap — especially corners
2. Place inside a snug-fitting cardboard box — the product should not shift when you shake the box
3. If the box is too large, fill void with bubble wrap or scrunched paper
4. Seal all seams with heavy-duty packing tape — at minimum top, bottom, and both side seams
5. For Japanese sealed product: extra care with shrink wrap — double-wrap if the shrink is loose or delicate
6. Include the **HOKO10 package insert card** before sealing
7. Mark **FRAGILE** if the set contains anything breakable (e.g. premium collection with metal coins/figures)

### What NOT to do
- Never ship a sealed booster box in a soft satchel
- Never let the product rattle or shift inside the outer box
- Never use a box that's significantly larger than the product — too much void space = product shifts in transit

---

## 3. Sealed Packs / Small Items

### Materials needed
- Rigid bubble mailer (minimum — not a soft padded envelope for singles)
- Toploaders or cardboard backing for individual packs if sending unboxed
- Packing tape

### Method

1. For individual packs: slide into a toploader or between two pieces of cardboard for rigidity
2. Place in a rigid bubble mailer — seal and tape the top
3. For small lots (2-5 packs): wrap together in bubble wrap, place in rigid bubble mailer
4. Include **HOKO10 package insert card**

## 5. Dispatch standard

- **Same business day** for orders placed before [owner sets cutoff time — suggest 1pm AEST]

- **Free for orders over $100 AUD**
- **Tracking number** sent to buyer immediately after dispatch (auto via Shopify/eBay)

---

## 6. Fragile / high-value orders ($300+)

For any order over $300 AUD:
- Add a second layer of external bubble wrap around the entire box
- Consider a stiffer outer box if the item box is thin
- Take a photo of the packed parcel before sealing — useful if a damage claim arises
- Flag the order as high-value in your dispatch records
