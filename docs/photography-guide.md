# Photography Guide

Photo standards for HOKO Collectables listings and social content.
Consistent, clean photos build trust and reduce INAD claims.

---

## Guiding principle

Show exactly what the buyer is getting — no flattering angles that hide flaws, no stock images for actual sold items. The photo is a contract.

---

## Equipment

**Minimum setup (what you likely already have):**
- Smartphone with a decent camera (iPhone 12+ or equivalent)
- Natural daylight or a ring light / LED panel
- White or light grey background (foam board or blank wall)
- For slabs: a lightbox or white foam board reflectors on two sides

**Recommended additions:**
- Lightbox (30–40cm cube) — removes shadows from slabs and small items
- Card stand or slab stand — keeps item upright for front-on shots
- Tripod or phone mount — eliminates blur on macro shots

---

## Slab photography

### Required shots (minimum for every slab listing)

| Shot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Front — full slab | Entire slab in frame, label readable |
| Front — card close-up | Card fills most of frame, grade label visible |
| Back — full slab | Show back of card clearly |
| Cert label | PSA/BGS/CGC cert number clearly readable |

### Additional shots (for slabs over $200)

| Shot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Slab edges | All 4 edges to show no cracks or tampering |
| Angle shot | 45-degree angle showing depth of slab |
| Scale reference | Slab next to a coin or ruler (optional — helps buyers gauge size) |

### Slab photography tips
- Shoot in a lightbox or next to a window — direct overhead light creates glare on the case
- Place white foam board or a sheet of white paper on the opposite side to the light — bounces light and kills shadows
- Hold phone parallel to the slab face (not angled) — keystoning makes the card look trapezoidal
- Zoom slightly rather than getting close — close shots distort edges
- Turn off flash — always causes glare on the slab surface
- Clean the slab with a microfibre cloth before shooting — fingerprints show up under listing lights
- For BGS slabs: photograph the sub-grade card insert if it's included

## Sealed product photography

### Required shots (minimum for every sealed listing)

| Shot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Front — full box/pack | Entire item in frame, artwork clearly visible |
| Back | Show back of box/pack |
| Top/bottom edges | Confirm seal is intact |
| Side panels | Show language / set details for TCG products |

### Additional shots (for sealed over $150)

| Shot | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Seal close-up | Show factory seal strip clearly |
| Any visible damage | If there are any dents, seam pulls, or wear — must be clearly shown |

### Sealed photography tips
- For ETB-sized boxes and booster boxes: shoot on a flat surface at eye level, not from above
- Show the seal clearly — buyers want to see it's untampered
- If there's a Korean or Japanese print: include a shot of the language marking on the box
- If the box is in any way not mint (dent, seam stress, etc.) — photograph it and describe it in the listing. Not photographing damage = future INAD claim.
- Avoid photographing on coloured surfaces — use white or neutral grey

## Social media photography

Social photos serve a different purpose to listing photos — they need to be visually interesting, not just accurate. More flexibility here.

### Instagram feed posts
- Flat lay: item on white or dark background with props (other slabs, packs, HOKO branding element)
- Stack shots: small fan of slabs or packs
- Hero shot: single item with dramatic side lighting
- Haul shot: full spread of new stock on a table

### Instagram Stories / Reels
- Unboxing: start with sealed outer packaging, show each layer
- Pull reveal: keep card face-down, flip to camera
- Slab inspection: slow rotation front to back under good light

### Photography tips for social
- Shoot in portrait (9:16) for Reels/Stories, square or landscape for feed posts
- Warm-toned light (tungsten or sunset) looks more collector-authentic than clinical white
- Real background (desk, card binder, shelves) is more relatable than a plain background for social
- Text overlays should not cover the card or grade label

---

## Editing

**For listings:**
- Adjust exposure and white balance only — do not add filters or increase saturation
- The photo should look like how the item actually looks under normal indoor light
- Crop to remove excess background — item should fill 70–80% of the frame
- Listing platforms compress images — shoot at maximum quality and let the platform handle it

**For social:**
- Light editing is fine — Lightroom Mobile presets, Instagram native filters (used sparingly)
- Don't over-saturate — oversaturated card colours can look deceptive
- Always add HOKO watermark or overlay on social posts featuring high-value items

---

## File naming convention

When saving listing photos locally before upload:

```
[GRADE]-[CERT]-[CARD_NAME]-[SHOT_TYPE].[EXT]

Examples:
PSA9-12345678-charizard-base-front.jpg
BGS9.5-87654321-pikachu-ex-back.jpg
SEALED-SV8-twilightmasquerade-etb-front.jpg
```

## Minimum photo counts by product type

| Product type | eBay minimum | Shopify minimum | High-value (>$200) |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| PSA/BGS/CGC slab | 4 | 4 | 6+ |
| Sealed booster box | 4 | 3 | 5+ |
| ETB / special set box | 4 | 3 | 5+ |
| Booster pack / blister | 3 | 2 | 4+ |
