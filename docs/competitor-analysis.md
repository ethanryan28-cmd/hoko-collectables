# Competitor Analysis

HOKO Collectables market positioning reference.
Review quarterly or when the market shifts significantly.

---

## HOKO's Position

| Dimension | HOKO Position |
|---|---|
| Product scope | PSA/BGS/CGC graded slabs + sealed TCG only |
| Grader policy | PSA, BGS, CGC only — no HGA, GMG, unverified |
| Pricing strategy | Replacement cost, never undercutting |
| Moat | Service, curation, collector trust — not price |
| Buyback | 75-80% FMV, rigid rules, bank/storage facility meetings only |
| Voice | Collector-to-collector, AU English, honest |
| Platforms | Shopify, eBay, Whatnot, TikTok/Instagram |

---

## Competitive Landscape

### Segment 1 — Large AU Marketplaces

Examples: Trading card marketplaces, large eBay Power Sellers

**Their strengths:** Volume, SEO, brand recognition
**Their weaknesses:** Impersonal, mixed grader quality, no curation
**HOKO's edge:** Collector expertise, grader standards, trusted voice

---

### Segment 2 — Instagram/TikTok Resellers

Examples: Individual sellers with social followings

**Their strengths:** Community feel, fast-moving content
**Their weaknesses:** Inconsistent grader standards, no formal buyback, no fixed platform
**HOKO's edge:** Shopify + eBay presence, formal buyback process, clear policies

---

### Segment 3 — Whatnot Sellers

Examples: Regular Whatnot streamers

**Their strengths:** Live engagement, fast sales
**Their weaknesses:** Variable card quality, often include ungraded or HGA product
**HOKO's edge:** PSA/BGS/CGC only, no fluff, collector-grade curation

---

### Segment 4 — LGS (Local Game Stores)

Examples: Card shops in Melbourne

**Their strengths:** Walk-in traffic, local presence, new product
**Their weaknesses:** Limited slab selection, often no buyback, RRP or above on sealed
**HOKO's edge:** Specialist slab range, buyback service, online reach beyond local

## Pricing Benchmark

Monitor these sources for market pricing:

| Source | What to Track |
|---|---|
| eBay AU sold listings | PSA 10 comps for core slabs, sealed prices |
| eBay US sold listings | USD → AUD for international benchmark |
| TCGPlayer | MTG sealed pricing |
| Whatnot recent sales | Live show prices for comparable slabs |
| Pokémon Centre AU | New sealed RRP (for ETBs, UPC, booster boxes) |

**Rule:** HOKO prices at replacement cost. If eBay AU comps are $300, HOKO prices at $300.
Never price below market to drive volume — moat is service, not price.

---

## What HOKO Is Not

Being clear about what HOKO doesn't do is part of the brand:

- Not a bulk singles seller
- Not a mystery pack dealer
- Not an HGA or GMG buyer/seller
- Not a 'cheapest price' store
- Not a general TCG store — specialist only

## Quarterly Review Questions

Run through these each quarter:

1. Has anything changed in the AU market that affects pricing or sourcing?
2. Are there new competitors in the graded slab space worth tracking?
3. Is HOKO's buyback rate (75-80%) still competitive vs other buyers?
4. Are any grading companies gaining or losing credibility (watch HGA, GMG)?
5. What are buyers saying they can't find — any gaps HOKO could fill?
6. Is the collector-to-collector voice still resonating on social?
