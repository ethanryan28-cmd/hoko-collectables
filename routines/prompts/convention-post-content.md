# Prompt: Convention / Card Show Post-Content

Use when: you've just come back from a card show or convention and want to turn the experience into a week of content.

Run this right after the show while it's fresh — or use voice dump if you're in the car.

---

## Inputs (fill in before running)

```
Event name: [e.g. Sydney TCG Expo]
Date: [DD/MM/YYYY]
Location: [suburb/venue]
Overall vibe: [busy/quiet/great/average]

What sold well (list): 
  - [item]: [approx price]
  - [item]: [approx price]

What didn't move:
  - [item or category]

Any pulls / interesting cards you saw or opened (if you did any on the day):
  - [card + context]

Buyback enquiries from the day:
  - [number of enquiries] — [any details]

Anything funny or memorable that happened:
  - [optional]

Any photos/videos taken: [yes / no — describe what you got]

New arrivals or stock coming in as a result of the show:
  - [if any]
```

---

## What to produce

### 1. Show recap post (IG / TikTok caption)

Format: hook → what happened → takeaway → CTA

**Template:**
> [Show name] — done. [1-2 sentence summary of vibe]. [Specific highlight — what sold, what you saw, best moment]. [Teaser for what's coming to the store next]. Follow @hokocollectables — new stock drops this week.

AU English, collector-to-collector, honest. No marketing hype.

---

### 2. "What sold?" post

Great transparent content — collectors love knowing what moves at shows.

> "Quick breakdown from [show name]: [item] went first, [item] went second. [Any surprise sellers]. [What didn't move and why — honest]. Shows are always a good read on what the market actually wants vs what lists well online."

Tone: insider knowledge, not bragging.

---

### 3. Pulls of the day post (if applicable)

Only if you actually opened product at the show. Don't fabricate.

> "Did a couple [set name] boxes at the show today. Hits: [pulls]. [Reaction]. [Context — keep or slab or list?]."

---

### 4. "New stock incoming" post

If you sourced or took orders at the show:

> "Picked up a few things at [show name] that'll be listing this week: [vague list — don't reveal exact details until listed]. Online store gets first dibs — link in bio."

---

### 5. Content calendar for the week post-show

Map out 5-7 posts from this one event:

| Day | Content | Format |
|-----|---------|--------|
| Day 1 (today) | Show recap | IG post + Story |
| Day 2 | What sold post | IG post |
| Day 3 | Pulls post (if applicable) | IG Reel |
| Day 4 | New stock tease | IG Story |
| Day 5 | New stock live | IG post |
| Day 6 | Behind the scenes / table setup photo | IG Story |
| Day 7 | Buyback reminder (if any enquiries came from show) | IG post |

---

### 6. Buyback follow-up (if enquiries came in)

For any collection sellers who chatted at the show:

Prompt: "I spoke to [number] people at [show] about buybacks. Draft follow-up messages to send to each one — use the buyback-dm-reply.md template, personalising the opener to reference meeting at the show."

Example opener:
> "Hey [name], great chatting at [show] today — here's the intake form I mentioned. If you can send through a photo of the collection + a list, I'll get back to you within 48 hours with a quote."

---

## Tone reminders

- Honest about what worked and what didn't — this is a strength, not weakness
- Don't oversell the event — if it was quiet, say so ("quieter day than expected, but good conversations")
- Use the photos you actually took — don't use stock images
- Keep all content connected to the brand: sealed TCG + graded slabs, Melbourne-based, collector-run
