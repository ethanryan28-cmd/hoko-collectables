# Buyback / DM Reply Drafter — HOKO Collectables

Use when someone DMs or emails asking about selling their collection.
Paste their message below and answer the inputs. Claude will draft a reply.

## Inputs

- **Their message (paste it):**

[PASTE MESSAGE]

- **What they're offering:** (cards / sealed / slabs / mixed / unknown)
- **Any prices or grades mentioned?** (yes/no — if yes, paste what they said)
- **Graders mentioned?** (PSA / BGS / CGC / HGA / GMG / ungraded / unknown)
- **Fits buyback criteria?** (yes / no / maybe — see rules below)
- **Fits consignment criteria?** (yes / no / maybe)
- **Any red flags?** (pushy, unrealistic price, loose singles only, damaged sealed, unverified graders)

---

## Buyback rules (never bend these)

- **What HOKO buys:** factory-sealed TCG product + PSA/BGS/CGC graded slabs $100+ each only
- **What HOKO does NOT buy:** loose singles, binders, bulk, damaged sealed, slabs under $100, HGA/GMG/unverified-grader slabs
- **Price:** 75-80% of current fair market value — no exceptions
- **Meeting location:** local bank branch or nominated storage facility, business hours only. No home pickups. No after-hours.
- **Photo + typed inventory required** before any quote (one wide collection photo + typed list)
- **Quote validity:** 48 hours from issue
- **Payment:** bank transfer on the day. No PayPal, no cash, no instalments.

---

## Output

Draft a reply that:

1. Is warm but professional — collector talking to another collector
2. States clearly what HOKO can and cannot buy from this seller (based on what they described)
3. If it fits: explains next steps (photo + typed list, 48hr quote, bank branch meetup)
4. If it doesn't fit: declines politely, explains why briefly, does NOT suggest HGA/GMG slabs are acceptable under any circumstances
5. If it partially fits: notes what qualifies and what doesn't — offer to quote on the qualifying items only
6. If it's borderline consignment territory: note it as an option ("we also offer consignment for the right items — happy to explain more if you're interested") but do NOT send the full consignment intake email yet
7. Ends with a clear call to action (what should they do next?)

**Tone:** direct, honest, warm. Not stiff or corporate. Not overly casual. Like a reply from someone who knows their stuff and respects the seller's time.
**Length:** short — mobile-readable. No essays.
**Language:** AU English.

---

## Example scenarios

### Scenario A — Fits perfectly
Seller has 3 PSA 9 slabs, all Pokemon, all above $200 each. Send them the full next-step instructions: photo + typed list, 48hr quote, bank branch meetup.

### Scenario B — Partial fit
Seller has a mix of slabs + singles binder. Draft a reply that says: "We can quote on the slabs — not the binder, that's outside what we buy. Happy to look at the slabs if you can send photos and a typed list."

### Scenario C — Doesn't fit at all
Seller has loose singles and an HGA slab. Decline politely. Don't lecture. One sentence on why.

### Scenario D — Consignment candidate
Seller wants "full retail" for a valuable slab and is in no rush. Mention buyback at 75-80% FMV AND float consignment as an alternative ("you list it through us, we take 20% when it sells, you keep 80%"). Don't send the full consignment intake email — just gauge interest.
