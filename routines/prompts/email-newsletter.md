# Email Newsletter — Monthly Prompt

Use this prompt each month to generate a newsletter for HOKO Collectables subscribers.
Always review and personalise the draft before sending — never send AI output directly.

---

## Inputs to Gather Before Running

Before using the prompt, collect the following:

| Input | Example |
|---|---|
| Month/Year | June 2026 |
| New arrivals (list) | PSA 10 Charizard VMAX, Scarlet & Violet Base ETB x4 |
| Recent sells / highlights | PSA 9 Lugia sold in 2 hours |
| Any restocks | Paradox Rift booster boxes back in stock |
| Events attended or upcoming | Melbourne Card Show 14 June |
| Platform news | Whatnot show scheduled for 28 June |
| Promo or discount | None (standard month) |
| Tone direction | Excited / Calm / Milestone |

---

## The Prompt

```
You are writing a monthly email newsletter for HOKO Collectables, a Melbourne-based
collectables business run by Ethan. HOKO sells PSA/BGS/CGC graded slabs and sealed
TCG product (Pokemon, MTG, One Piece, Dragon Ball Super). Tone: collector-to-collector,
honest, no marketing hype, AU English.

Write a newsletter for [MONTH YEAR] using the following inputs:

New arrivals: [LIST]
Recent highlights: [LIST]
Restocks: [LIST]
Events: [LIST]
Upcoming platform activity: [LIST]
Promo/discount: [LIST OR 'none']
Tone direction: [EXCITED / CALM / MILESTONE]

Output the following:

1. SUBJECT LINE OPTIONS (3 variations — short, punchy, no clickbait)
2. PREVIEW TEXT (1 line, under 90 chars, complements subject)
3. OPENING PARAGRAPH (2-3 sentences, personal, from Ethan, no salesy language)
4. WHAT'S IN STOCK section (brief bullet points per item — name, grade/set, 1 line why it's notable)
5. STORY / HIGHLIGHT OF THE MONTH (3-4 sentences — something real that happened this month)
6. UPCOMING section (events, Whatnot shows, anything the subscriber should know about)
7. CLOSING PARAGRAPH (warm, personal, collector voice — never 'Thanks for your support')
8. CTA (one call to action only — link placeholder, no pressure language)

Rules:
- AU English throughout
- Never use: 'Don't miss out', 'Limited time', 'Act now', 'Exclusive deal'
- Never invent card names, prices, or events not in the inputs
- HOKO10 discount code is for acquisition only — never include in newsletters
- Do not mention consignment — it is not yet launched
- Keep total email under 350 words
- Write as Ethan, not as a brand
```

---

## Example Output Structure

### Subject line options
- June drop — new slabs + sealed just landed
- What came in this month (and what sold fast)
- June at HOKO — highlights, restocks, and what's coming

### Preview text
New PSA slabs, restocked ETBs, and a Whatnot show on the 28th.

### Opening paragraph
Hey — June was a good one. Picked up a few slabs I've been chasing for a while, got
some sealed back in stock, and have a Whatnot show lined up for the end of the month.
Here's what's happening.

*(continue through all 8 sections)*

---

## What NOT to Include

- Fabricated arrivals or prices
- HOKO10 code in newsletters
- Consignment offers
- Countdown timers or urgency language
- Excessive exclamation marks
- 'Thanks for your support' or similar corporate sign-offs
- Generic stock imagery descriptions

---

## Platform Notes

**Klaviyo (Shopify):** Keep subject under 60 chars. Preview text fills automatically.
**eBay newsletter:** Not currently used — all newsletter activity via Shopify/Klaviyo.
**Frequency:** Monthly, sent first week of the following month.

---

## Tone Reminders

- Write like you're emailing a mate who collects
- Don't oversell — if something is notable, say why briefly and move on
- Avoid buzzwords: 'iconic', 'legendary', 'fire', 'grail' (unless quoting a buyer)
- Short sentences over long ones
- Use 'I' not 'we' — HOKO is a one-person business
